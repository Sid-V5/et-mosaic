SECTION 1: WHAT ET MOSAIC ACTUALLY DOES
Read this section before anything else. This is the honest, precise answer to 'what does it do?' — including what it does NOT do.

What it does
ET Mosaic practises Mosaic Theory — the CFA-recognised technique of connecting independent pieces of public information to form a complete intelligence picture that no single source reveals. It applies this to ET journalism + NSE market data + technical indicators, running autonomously every 15 minutes.

Specifically, every 15 minutes the system:
Ingests all new articles from 6 ET RSS channels (India primary) and 2 global macro feeds (context only)
Extracts entities, events, sentiment, and signal keywords from each article using LLaMA 3.1 8B
Runs technical analysis (RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands, 200-DMA) on the companies mentioned
Finds cross-source, cross-modal connections: articles from different channels + NSE bulk deals + TA indicators all pointing at the same company
Verifies each connection against live NSE data (bulk deals, insider trades, price/volume, options OI)
Detects sector contagion — is this one company or the start of a sector event?
Generates plain-English warning cards with full citation chains
Generates 30-second Hindi audio briefs for each signal
Ranks signals by portfolio relevance, freshness, and historical accuracy

What it does NOT do
It does NOT predict stock prices. No system can do this reliably.
It does NOT give financial advice or recommend trades.
It does NOT use insider information. Every data point is 100% public.
It does NOT summarise news articles. It finds patterns across them.

What 'signals' means — exactly
A signal is a cross-source convergence: when 3+ independent data sources from different channels all show concerning patterns about the same company within 7 days. For example:

Article 1 (ET Corporate): 'Vedanta CFO resigns after 4 years' | Article 2 (ET Economy): 'LME Zinc falls 8% on China slowdown' | NSE Bulk Deals: promoter sold ₹180Cr in 3 sessions | Technical: RSI overbought + MACD bearish cross below 200-DMA | Confidence: 79% | Historical accuracy of this pattern: 73% | What to watch: NSE bulk deal data Thursday.

The signal card does NOT say 'sell Vedanta.' It says: these 4 independent data points converged on Vedanta this week. Historically, this exact pattern preceded significant drawdowns 73% of the time. Here are the sources. The decision is yours.

That is Mosaic Theory. That is what Bloomberg charges ₹27 lakh/year for. ET Mosaic makes it free.
